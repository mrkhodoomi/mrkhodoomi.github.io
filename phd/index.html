    Design of peer-to-peer energy trading with battery storage under uncertainty conditions

    In response to the challenges posed by rising electricity prices, lower Feed-in-tariffs, and increased dispersed generation from renewable energy sources, a new method of energy trading is "peer-to-peer energy trade". This method refers to the direct energy trade between consumers and producers in distribution networks. On the other hand, the development of distributed energy sources and the increase in the number of households that supply their household electricity with small-scale power generation systems; brings many opportunities and challenges to the energy market. Opportunity to create new energy trading models that offer residential consumers-producers different solutions for using energy generated. Peer-to-peer energy trading is an effective solution in order to motivate consumers to produce clean energy as well as to make the use of distributed energy sources more cost-effective and maximize the financial efficiency of micro-residential grids.

    In this research, the power supply chain network has been designed for residential peers that use various facilities of distributed generation as well as battery storage. In addition to peers, the electricity grid and the central battery storage have been considered. Since the demand of each peer, as well as the amount of renewable energy generation, is not certain, the Robust Optimization method has been used to minimize the costs of each unit. In addition to distribution network design, the discussion of energy pricing transferred in the peer-to-peer energy trading has been calculated in order to maximize the peers' profits through the Nash Bargaining Game Theory method. The results show that the use of peer-to-peer energy trading can not only minimize costs but also boost benefit peers.
